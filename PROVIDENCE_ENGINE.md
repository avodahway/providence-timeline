# Providence Timeline Engine

Providence Timeline treats every saved page as a coordinate in a larger living atlas.

## Page Object

The visible writing fields remain simple: date, title, narrative, season, story arc, scripture, people, tags, location, and reflection.

The expanded atlas layer is stored in `entries_v2.atlas` as JSON:

- `prayer`
- `organizations`
- `projects`
- `themes`
- `emotions`
- `questions`
- `answered_prayers`
- `documents`
- `photos`
- `field_notes`
- `visibility`
- `current`
- `landmark`
- `importance`
- `confidence`
- `follow_up`
- `latitude`
- `longitude`
- `suggested_routes`

Latitude and longitude are symbolic chart coordinates. They are not GPS.

## Structured Extraction

Every saved page also stores `entries_v2.structured_data`. This preserves extracted information without replacing the original journal text:

- people
- family members
- organizations
- churches
- ministries
- places, cities, and countries
- scripture references
- prayer requests
- answered prayers
- questions
- decisions
- provisions
- emotions
- projects
- themes
- recurring ideas
- currents
- story arcs
- seasons
- important dates
- keywords

The current extraction is intentionally conservative and local. It does not infer God's intent or create theological meaning.

## Routes

Routes are inferred from shared signals:

- people
- places
- organizations
- projects
- themes
- emotions
- scripture
- tags
- seasons
- currents
- story arcs
- date anniversaries
- repeated text echoes

Manual connections remain in `connections`. Suggested routes are generated by the client engine and can later be promoted into durable routes.

Each page stores `relationship_edges` with:

- source page
- target page
- relationship label
- confidence bucket: Low, Medium, High, Very High
- internal strength score
- evidence signals

The score is saved for future use but should not be shown directly to users yet.

Allowed labels stay cautious:

- Possible relationship
- Shared theme
- Shared people
- Shared prayer
- Repeated question
- Potential story arc

The engine should never state that God connected two events. It only identifies possible patterns worth later reflection.

## Providence Map Data

Each page stores `map_data` for future map views:

- nodes
- edges
- suggested routes
- clusters
- story arcs
- recurring themes
- timeline coordinates

Future AI features should read `structured_data`, `relationship_edges`, and `map_data` before rereading full journal text.

## Living Stories

Living Stories are recurring people, families, churches, ministries, businesses, places, projects, seasons, or prayer burdens that appear across pages.

They are stored in `entries_v2.living_stories` as JSON:

- `name`
- `type`
- `notes`

The engine also detects recurring Living Stories from existing page fields such as people, organizations, places, projects, story arcs, seasons, and intercession targets. A Living Story is a shared story, not a task list or contact record.

Living Story map language must stay cautious:

- Shared story
- Possible relationship
- Still unfolding
- Worth revisiting
- Later reflection

## Intercession

Intercession is optional. It marks a page as part of someone else's story intersecting the user's story.

It is stored in `entries_v2.intercession` as JSON:

- `enabled`
- `target`
- `prayer`
- `concern`
- `follow_up_date`
- `status`
- `notes`

Allowed statuses:

- Waiting
- Continuing
- Changed
- Answered
- Needs Follow-up
- Closed

The app may gently surface dated intercession pages with language such as "Worth revisiting" or "Has anything changed?" It must not say "overdue" or imply failure.

The engine must never mark an intercession as answered unless the user explicitly chooses Answered.

## Currents

Currents are recurring invisible themes. They are detected from seasons, story arcs, tags, themes, emotions, and the explicit `current` field.

## Constellations

Constellations are groups of pages connected by repeated currents. They are intentionally provisional until enough pages exist to make a pattern visible.

## Landmarks

Landmarks mark permanent significance: marriage, child born, salvation, calling, business launch, mission trip, answered prayer, provision, and turning points.
